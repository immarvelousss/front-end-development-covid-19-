The Moderna/Spikevax Vaccine


The Creator of the Moderna/Spikevax Vaccine

  • Individuals must be between 6 months to 4 years old, or 18 years old and above to receive the Moderna/Spikevax vaccine.

  • The Moderna/Spikevax vaccine has messenger RNA (mRNA), which aids in the production of defense by your immune system.

  • The vaccine will help to lower the risk of infection and severe illness from COVID-19 and provides good protection against current virus variants.

  • The vaccine is advised as a booster dose starting approximately 5 months following the last dose for individuals aged 18 years and older.



About

The American company Moderna, the American National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases (NIAID), and the Biomedical Advanced Research and Development Authority produced the Moderna COVID-19 vaccine, which is marketed under the trade name Spikevax (BARDA). It is approved for use in people six months of age or older, twelve years of age, or eighteen years of age or older, depending on the jurisdiction.

It offers defense against COVID-19, a virus infection brought on by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It is intended to be given intramuscularly in two or three doses of 0.5 ml each, spaced at least 28 days apart.

It is an mRNA vaccine made of lipid nanoparticle-coated nucleoside-modified mRNA (modRNA), which encodes the SARAS-CoV-2 spike protein. In many nations, it has some kind of authorization for use.



Timeline

Year 2020

Moderna announced the creation of an RNA vaccine, known as mRNA-1273, to generate immunity to SARS-CoV-2 in January 2020.

The Moderna COVID-19 vaccine was being considered by a number of nations as of December 2020 for emergency authorization or approval, which would allow for a quick rollout of the vaccine in the United Kingdom, the European Union (EU), Canada, and the United States.

The US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) granted an Emergency Use Authorization (EUA) for the Moderna COVID-19 vaccine on December 18, 2020, for those who are 18 years of age or older. The FDA has given its approval to this Moderna product, the company's first. The EUA was extended to include individuals from birth to the age of sixteen in June 2022.


Year 2021

In the year 2021, the Moderna COVID-19 vaccine was authorised for emergency use in various countries including the Philippines, India, Vietnam, and Japan.


Year 2022

Bivalent vaccines containing elasomeran/elasomeran 0-omicron (Spikevax Bivalent Zero/Omicron) were authorized for use as booster doses in people 18 years of age and older in the United Kingdom, Switzerland, Australia, Canada, the European Union, and the United States in August and September 2022.

Omicron BA.4/BA.5 variant is the basis for the second component of the bivalent vaccine version used in the United States (mRNA-1273.222), whereas Omicron BA.1 variant is the basis for the second component of the bivalent vaccine version used in other countries (mRNA-1273.214).



Adverse Effects

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the vaccine's adverse event (AE) profile "did not suggest any specific safety concerns" and that "the safety data supported a favorable safety profile." The most frequent side effects were arthralgia, myalgia (muscle pain), lethargy, headaches, and pain at the injection site (joint pain).

The US CDC confirmed in June 2021 that 13 out of every million young people, mostly male and older than 16, who received the Moderna or Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine develop myocarditis or pericarditis. Most affected people bounce back fast with the right care and rest. The vaccinated limb may experience significant swelling as additional side effects.
