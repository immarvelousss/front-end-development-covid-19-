Summary of COVID-19 Vaccines Available in Singapore

For easy referencing

Age Group What is it? Benefits Booster Jab
Pfizer-BioNTech/Comirnaty > 6 months old Messenger RNA (mRNA) which helps the immune system to produce protection. Good protection against the current virus variations and lowers the risk of infection and serious sickness from COVID-19.
  • Timeframe: 5 months after last vaccine
  • Age Group: > 5 years
Moderna/Spikevax 6 months - 4 years OR > 18 years
  • Timeframe: 5 months after the last vaccine
  • Age Group: > 18 years
Novavax/Nuvaxovid > 18 years purified copies of a protein found on the COVID-19 virus (SARS-CoV-2 recombinant spike protein) which is used to stimulate the body to develop protection against the actual COVID-19 virus. Reduces the risks of infection and severe disease from COVID-19.

Acceptable alternative to mRNA vaccines for use as a booster dose.

  • Timeframe: 5 months after the last vaccine
  • Age Group: > 18 years
Sinovac-CoronaVac Taken only if unable to complete vaccination with mRNA vaccines and Novavax/Nuvaxovid due to an allergic reaction.

Inactivated COVID-19 virus which stimulates the body to produce protection against the actual COVID-19 virus. 		Protection against severe and fatal COVID-19 may be high, but protection against virus variants suck as Omicron may be lower.
  • Timeframe: 3 months after lasat Sinovac-CoronaVac
  • Age Group: > 18 years

Having Minimum Protection against COVID-19

For minimum protection, persons aged 5 years and above should complete, as applicable,

  • 3 doses of Pfizer-BioNTech/Comirnaty, Moderna/Spikevax or Novavax/Nuvaxovid; or,
  • 4 doses of Sinovac-CoronaVac

These would be doses that were previuosly referred to as the primary vaccination series and the first booster.


Keeping Up-to-Date

Individuals should take an additional booster dose of the COVID-19 vaccine between five months and one year from their last vaccine dose in order to maintain their level of protection:

Persons who are

  • aged 12 years and above
  • living in aged care facilities
  • medically vulnerable and at higher risk of severe disease

Side Effects of Vaccinations


Side Effects How to Manage
Pain, redness, swelling at the injection site Those with fever are advised to self-isolate at home until the fever subsides.

Paracetamol 1 to 2 tablets for the other side effects every 6 hours for adults or dosed accordingly to the child's weight as needed.
Fever, chills
Headache, muscle pain, joint pain
Tiredness Rest
Lymph node swelling at neck or arms Usually gets better by itself in a week or so

See a doctor if:

  • The side effects persist or get worse
  • The fever persists for more than 48 hours (2 days)
  • Respiratory symptoms such as cough, runny nose, sore throat, shortness of breath or loss of sense of taste and smell develop
  • Heart or chest symptoms such as chest pain, shortness of breath or abnormal heart beats develop

In very rare cases, vaccines can cause a severe allergic reaction. Signs of a severe allergic reaction include difficulty breathing, swelling of your face, throat, eyes or lips, a fast heartbeat, dizziness and weakness, a bad rash all over your body. If you experience a severe allergic reaction, seek medical attention immediately. Call 995 or go to the nearest A&E immediately.
