Introduction

Below are the immediate actions that you should take if you are feeling unwell or when you found out you tested positive for covid. There is also information on what you should do during your covid phase, the meaning of your ART test results and procedures to follow upon knowing your condition.

You can also take a quiz at the end to find out which measures you should take!

Immediate Actions

When You Feel Unwell

  1. You do not need to go to hospitals to get tested for covid.
  2. Consult a doctor or request for a Telemedicine (TM) consultation from a TM provider.

Refer to protocol 1 below for more details.

When Tested Covid Positive

  1. Immediately stop going to work or school and cancel your social activities.
  2. Self-isolate for at leaast 72 hours at home and make as little contact with others as possible.

Refer to protocol 2 below for more details.

You should wear a mask immediately, and isolate yourself at home in a separate room to minimise interactions with the rest of the household, especially seniors.

If you are using a shared space, surfaces that you touch should be wiped down with disinfectant or bleach solution.

Maintain good personal hygiene by:

  • Washing your hands with soap and water frequently
  • Not sharing any food, crockery, utensils and other personal hygiene items

Monitor your temperature and symptoms. If your symptoms worsen, please call a healthcare professional or go visit a doctor.

In an emergency (e.g. chest pain, palpitations, shortness of breath etc.), please call 995 for an ambulance and inform the operator that you are COVID positive.

Protocol 1

For those that are feeling unwell.

Click here for low-risk individuals with mild symptoms (e.g. fever, cough, sore throat etc.)

  1. Your doctor will administer an ART test.
  2. If you test negative, your doctor will advise you accordingly. As a precaution, you should continue to monitor your health. If the symptoms continue or worsens the next day, it is advised to take another ART test.
  3. If you test positive, refer to Protocol 2.
  4. You will receive an SMS within 24 hours upon notifying the authorised personnel of your covid positive results. The SMS will contain a link to fill in a form.
  5. ART kits can be collected from nearby vending machines.
  6. Please bring along your NRIC/FIN as it will be used to scan at the vending machine to collect your ART kits.
  7. Non-residents are to self-procure ART kits which are widely available through local retail pharmacies. If staying in a hotel, please call hotel concierge for assistance.

Click here for high-risk individuals or those with severe significant symptoms

  1. Your doctor will administer an ART test and PCR swab.
  2. For patients with severe symptoms (e.g. chest pains, shortness of breath, prolonged fever), you will be conveyed to a hospital for further assessment.
  3. For the rest, your doctor will advise you to go back home for self-isolation in a room, until your PCR test results come out.
  4. If you test negative, your doctor will advise you on the next steps to take.
  5. If you test positive, MOH will contact you on your recovery journey. You will either enter the Home Recovery Programme by default, or if your home environment is not suitable, you will be assigned to appropriate care facilities

Protocol 2

For individuals who are well and tested positive for covid.

Click here for more information

  1. Self-isolate at home for at least 72 hours.
  2. If well, may exit self-isolation and resume normal activities upon a negative self-administered ART result.
  3. Those who continue to test ART positive to continue to self-isolate and self-test daily until:
    • They obtain a negative ART result
    • Until 12PM on Day 7 (for vaccinated individuals and children below 12 years old) or Day 14 (for unvaccinated/ partially vaccinated individuals aged 12 years old and above). Day 1 is when you first tested positive.
  4. No Medical Certificate (MC) will be issued from a doctor if there are no symptoms. If a doctor has assessed you to have mild symptoms, you will be issued an MC.
  5. Consult a doctor or call 995 in emergency situations if symptoms worsen or do not improve with time.

Protocol 3

For individuals who are identified by MOH as a close contact of an infected person

Click here for more information

  1. Take an ART self-test within 24 hours.
  2. Required to self-test with ART daily for 5 days. Only leave home with negative ART test.
