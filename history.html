History of COVID-19

As COVID-19 continues to spread quickly, the Singapore Government has imposed a more unique way to curb the spread of the COVID-19 virus to one another. Circuit Breaker was imposed, which is then further broken down into 3 phases to safely transistion Singapore back to its normal life. During these phases, Singaporeans had to follow some rules imposed such as always wearing of masks, and only being able to hang out in groups of certain number (depending on which phase it was).

Read more below to find out more about what happened in Singapore during the world-wide pandemic.



Timeline

  • 31 Dec 2019

    Wuhan virus

    A cluster of cases of pneumonia in Wuhan, Hubei Province has been reported by Wuhan Municpal Health Commission, China. As it was still early, it was then subbed as the "Wuhan Virus"

  • 23 Jan 2020

    First imported case in Singpaore

    National lockdown happens worldwide. School were all changed to Home-Based Learning (HBL), Work-from-home was introduced and rules were enforced to ensure that all people were to wear their masks.

  • 23 Mar 2020

    First COVID-19 death in Singpaore

    A 66 year-old Chinese national from Wuhan who arrived in Singagore with his family on 20 Jan 2020 and was confirmed by the Ministry of Health (MOH) to be an imported case of Covid-19.

  • 23 Mar 2020

    First 2 COVID-19 deaths in Singapore

    The 1st patient was a 75 year-old female Singapore Citizen with chronic heart disease and hypertension died at 7.52am The 2nd patient was a 64 year-old male Indonesian national who was admitted into Singapore in critical condition to intensiva care at NCID on 13 Mar 2020 and caught the virus the next day. He died at 10.15am.

  • 07 Apr 2020

    Circuit Breaker Starts

    On 3rd April 2020, Prime Minister Lee Hsien Long has announced that a circuit breaker will be implemented to help reduce the risk of a big outbreak occurring. Masks were worn at all times with reusable mask given out to households. Most workplaces (except essential services) are closed and schools moved to Home-Based Learning (HBL).

  • 02 June 2020

    Circuit Breaker Ends, Phase 1 starts

    Although Circuit Breaker ended, it did not mean that there was freedom, a loosened form of Heightened Alert and Safe Reopening was implemented.

  • 19 Jun 2020 - 17 July 2020

    Phase 2

    On 15 June 2020, the Multi-Ministry Taskforce announced that Singapore will start Phase 2 on 19 June 2020. This is due to the number of COVID cases generally remaining stable, no new large clusters and lower cases in migrant worker dormitories.

  • 1 Dec 2020

    Pfizer–BioNTech COVID‑19 vaccine

    The Pfizer–BioNTech COVID‑19 vaccine has been approved for use by the U.S. Food and Drug Adminstration (FDA).

  • 21 Dec 2020

    First Batch of Vaccines arrives in Singapore

    Singapore was the first nation in Asia to receive the COVID -19 vaccinations. Many were skeptical about the vaccines as it uses a new technology and no one knew what the long-term effect was.

  • 28 Dec 2020 - 8 May 2021

    Phase 3

    As Singapore has made good progress to support a further resumption of activities, on 14 Dec 2020, the Multi-Ministry Taskforce (MTF) will start Phase 3 of re-opening from 28 Dec 2020.

  • 3 Feb 2021

    Mordena COIVD-19 vaccine

    The Health Science Activity (HSA) announced that the Moderna COVID-19 vaccine had been approved for use in Singapore, with the first shipment expected to arrive on 17 Feb 2021.

  • 4 May 2021

    Implementation of SafeEntry

    To be able to track down clusters quickly, as well as to keep track of who has entered the specific place Singapore has implemented SafeEntry using the tokens (given by the government) and the TraceTogether app.

  • 22 May 2021 - 18 Aug 2021

    Back to Phase 2

    On 20 July 2021, it was announced that Singapore would go back to Phase 2 (Heightened Alert). Social gathering of group sizes were reduced to people and no dining-in at all F&B establishment to stop.

  • 10 Jun 2021

    ART Test Kits

    The use of certain Antigen Rapid Test (ART) Kits were authorised by Health Science Activity (HSA) under Pandemic Special Access Route (PSAR). The list is constantly updating.

  • 14 Jun 2021

    A New Phase

    On 10 June 2021, the Ministry of Health (MOH) has announced that they would ease on some of the restrictions that will take place in 2 phases (14 June 2021 and 21 June 2021).

  • 29 Mar 2022

    Ease on SMM Rules

    On 24 March 2022, it has be announced that there will be an ease on the SMM restrictions. One biggest restriction that was lifted was being able to not wear masks outdoor. Social gatherings also increased from 5 to 10 people.

  • 29 August 2022

    Further Ease on Mask Restrictions

    Mask-wearing was no longer needed from 29 Auguest 2022. This includes indoor and outdoor settings. The only exception to this were essential services carried out in enclosed areas such as medical places and any public transportation.
